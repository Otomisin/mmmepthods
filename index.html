The estimation is produced based on IOM Missing Migrants Project data on the number of dead/missing and the countries and regions of origin of those who have died, and the average household size as a proxy for family members of missing migrants. It should be noted that many family members live outside the household, so household numbers underestimate the number of people affected by the loss of a relative.

Data on dead or missing persons falls into three categories with regard to the specified area of origin: those for which the country of origin is known, those for which the region (but not country) of origin is known, and those for which no origin is known. We first consider the case of known countries of origin. We denote by $m_i$ the number of dead or missing persons from country $i$, by $h_i$ the average household size in country $i$, and by $h_i^{(n)}$ the average household size in province $n$ of country $i$, where the latter two variables were obtained from the global database of Esteve et al. (2024).1 The estimated number of household members of dead or missing persons with known origin country $i$ is then given by $m_i \cdot (h_i - 1)$, where the $-1$ reflects that we excluded the dead or missing persons themselves in the calculation. We defined the uncertainty of this estimate as $\hat{\sigma}_i = \varsigma_i \cdot m_i$, where $\varsigma_i$ denotes the standard deviation of $\bigl\{h_i^{(n)}\bigr\}_n$, which we use as a measure of the variation in household size within country $i$.

When only the region of origin, $j$, is known, we estimated the average household size associated with dead or missing persons of that region as

$$\hat{h}_j = \frac{\displaystyle\sum_{i \in C(j)} m_i \cdot h_i}{\displaystyle\sum_{i \in C(j)} m_i}$$

where $C(j)$ denotes the set of countries in region $j$. Thus, we assumed the distribution of origin countries of dead or missing persons whose origin is only known as region $j$ to be similar to the distribution of dead or missing persons from known origin countries within region $j$. The estimated number of household members of the $m_j$ dead or missing persons with origin region $j$ is then given by $m_j \cdot (\hat{h}_j - 1)$, and the corresponding uncertainty is given by

$$\hat{\sigma}_j = \sqrt{\frac{\displaystyle\sum_{i \in C(j)} m_i \cdot \Bigl(\hat{\sigma}_i^2 + \bigl(h_i - \hat{h}_j\bigr)^2\Bigr)}{\displaystyle\sum_{i \in C(j)} m_i}} \cdot m_j$$

When no origin is known, we estimated the average household size associated with dead or missing persons as

$$\hat{h}_{?} = \frac{\displaystyle\sum_{i} m_i \cdot h_i \;+\; \displaystyle\sum_{j} m_j \cdot \hat{h}_j}{\displaystyle\sum_{i} m_i \;+\; \displaystyle\sum_{j} m_j}$$

Note that this calculation accounts for both known origin countries and regions. The estimated number of household members of the $m_{?}$ dead or missing persons with unknown origin is then given by $m_{?} \cdot \bigl(\hat{h}_{?} - 1\bigr)$, with uncertainty

$$\hat{\sigma}_{?} = \sqrt{\frac{\displaystyle\sum_{i} m_i \Bigl(\hat{\sigma}_i^2 + \bigl(h_i - \hat{h}_{?}\bigr)^2\Bigr) \;+\; \displaystyle\sum_{j} m_j \Bigl(\hat{\sigma}_j^2 + \bigl(\hat{h}_j - \hat{h}_{?}\bigr)^2\Bigr)}{\displaystyle\sum_{i} m_i \;+\; \displaystyle\sum_{j} m_j}} \cdot m_{?}$$

Finally, the estimated total number of household members associated with the $\sum_i m_i + \sum_j m_j + m_{?} = 78{,}878$ dead or missing persons recorded globally is given by

$$\sum_{i} m_i \cdot (h_i - 1) \;+\; \sum_{j} m_j \cdot (\hat{h}_j - 1) \;+\; m_{?} \cdot (\hat{h}_{?} - 1)$$

with uncertainty of the number of household members were calculated analogously and are shown in the table below. Note that only the global estimate also includes persons with unknown origin.

$$\sqrt{\;\sum_{i} \hat{\sigma}_i^2 \;+\; \sum_{j} \hat{\sigma}_j^2 \;+\; \hat{\sigma}_{?}^2\;}$$
